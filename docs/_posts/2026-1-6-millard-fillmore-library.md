I *really* like Millard Fillmore, and I think it's a shame that people have to go to bespoke websites to find anything written by him, so I want to make this as a document containing *anything* I can get my hands on about him!

Please don't cite this page yourself, I will provide citations at the bottom for you go get this information for yourselves.

I will at some points add my own annotations linking to other websites that may explain what he was talking about. I will also put stars on items that I believe are important to know.

# Timeline

```mermaid
timeline
    title Chronology of Millard Fillmore
    section Early Life
        1800 : Born at Locke, now Summerhill, Cayuga county, New York.
        1815 : At work wool-carding and cloth-dressing.
        1818 : Taught school at Scott, New York.
             : Began reading law with Judge Wood at Montville, Cayuga county.
    section Lawyer Era
        1819 : Continued reading law at Montville.
        1821 : Moved to Aurora (now East Aurora, Erie county), New York.
        1822 : Read law in Buffalo, but lived in Aurora till spring, 1830.
        1823 : Practiced law in Aurora.
        1826 : Feb. 5. Married Abigail, daughter of the Rev. Lemuel Powers.
        1827 : Admitted to the bar, as attorney of the Supreme Court.
        1828 : April 25 - Millard Powers Fillmore born.
             : May 22. Delegate to the Erie county convention of the National Republicans; member of the county committee from Aurora; endorsed John Quincy Adams.
             : Nov. Elected to the New York Assembly, as candidate of the Anti-Masonic party.
        1829 : Admitted as counsellor, New York Supreme Court.
             : Reelected to New York Assembly.
        1830 : Formed law partnership with Joseph Clary.
             : Reelected to New York Assembly.
        1831 : Jan. 4. Took seat for third time in New York Assembly. Distinguished himself by drafting and advocating act to abolish imprisonment for debt; bill passed April 26, 1831.
        1832 : March 27 - Mary Abigail Fillmore born.
    section Congressional Era
        1832 : Elected Representative in Twenty-third Congress.
        1833 : Dec. 2. First took seat in House of Representatives.
        1834 : Law firm of Fillmore & Hall formed.
        1836 : Jan. 10. Law firm of Fillmore, Hall & Haven formed.
             : Oct. 4. Renominated for Representative in Twenty-fifth Congress; later elected.
        1838 : Reelected Representative in Twenty-sixth Congress.
        1840 : Reelected Representative in Twenty-seventh Congress; chairman of Ways and Means committee; "leader of the House."
        1842 : March 3 - Obtains appropriation for Morse's magnetic telegraph.
             : June 9. Made his famous tariff speech in the House.
             : Declined renomination to Congress.
        1844 : May. Candidate for Vice-President in the Whig National convention at Baltimore.
             : Sept. 11. Nominated for Governor of New York; defeated by Silas Wright.
    section University of Buffalo
        1847 : Oct. 6. Nominated for Comptroller, New York State, in the Whig State convention at Syracuse.
             : Nov. 2. Elected Comptroller, New York State.
        1848 : Jan. 1. Assumed office as Comptroller.
    section As Vice President
        1848 : June 9. Nominated for Vice-President by the Whig National convention.
             : Nov. Elected Vice-President.
        1849 : Feb. 20. Resigned as New York Comptroller.
             : March 4. Inaugurated Vice-President.
        1850 : April 3 - Address on RUles of Order in the Senate.
    section As President
        1850 : July 10. Took oath of office as President of the United States.
             : Sept. 18. Approved Fugitive Slave Act.
        1851 : July 4. Laid the corner-stone of the Capitol extension.
        1852 : June 16-21. Unsuccessful Whig candidate for Presidential nomination in the Whig National convention at Baltimore.
             : Nov. 13. Dispatch of Commodore Matthew Calbaith Perry to Japan.
        1853 : March 4. Retired from Presidency.
    section Post-Presidency
        1853 : March 30. Mrs. Fillmore died at Washington.
        1854 : Tour of southern and western States.
             : July 26 - Death of Mary Abigail Fillmore at East Aurora, New York.
        1855 : May 17. Sailed for Liverpool.
             : Declines D.C.L degree from Oxford University.
        1856 : Feb. 22. Nominated for President by the American party at Philadelphia.
             : May 21. Wrote his letter of acceptance at Paris.
             : June 22. Arrived in New York.
             : Nov. Overwhelmingly defeated in the election, receiving only the eight electoral votes of Maryland.
        1858 : Feb. 10. Married Mrs. Caroline C. McIntosh.
    section During the Civil War
        1862 : Chairman, Buffalo Committee of Public Defense.
             : One of the incorporators, Buffalo Fine Arts Academy.
             : May 20. Chosen president, Buffalo Historical Society, which he helped to found; reelected yearly, 1862-1867.
    section Post Civil War
        1865 : Dec. 8. Executed his last will and testament. Codicils dated September 19, 1868, and April 28, 1873.
        1866 : Visited Europe with his second wife.
             : July 13. Arrived in Buffalo, from his second european trip.
        1867 : First president, Buffalo Club, and one of its founders.
        1870 : President, Buffalo General Hospital.
        1870 : Trustee, Grosvenor Library, Buffalo.
        1874 : March 8. Died at his home in Buffalo.
```
